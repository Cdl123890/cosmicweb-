cosmic web

  1. Constellations

    Constellations are groups of stars that form patterns in the night sky.
    Different cultures have connected these stars to myths, animals, and stories, helping humans navigate
    and understand the universe.

  2. Orion – The Hunter

    descrpition:Orion is one of the most recognizable constellations, visible during winter in the northern hemisphere
    storyIn Greek mythology, Orion was a great hunter, placed in the sky by the gods
    ImportanceOrion helped ancient people track seasons and locate other stars.

  3. Cygnus – The Swan

    descrpition:Cygnus appears as a swan flying along the Milky Way, most visible in summer.
    storyOften linked to myths about swans in Greek and Roman culture.
    ImportanceCygnus helped sailors and travelers identify the Milky Way’s direction.

  4. Ursa Major – The Big Dipper

    descrpition:Ursa Major is one of the largest and most famous constellations, containing the Big Dipper asterism.
    storyKnown as the Great Bear in many cultures; myths vary from Greek to Native American traditions.
    ImportanceUsed for navigation, because the Big Dipper points roughly to the North Star (Polaris).

Other Notable Constellations

Cassiopeia: W-shaped, representing a queen in Greek mythology.
Leo: Lion-shaped, linked to heroic stories and seasonal tracking.
